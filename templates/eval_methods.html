What Do These Metrics Mean?

Silhouette Score

The Silhouette Score measures how similar a data point is to its own cluster compared to other clusters. It ranges from -1 to 1. A value near 1 means the object is well-matched to its own cluster and poorly matched to neighboring clusters. Values near 0 indicate overlapping clusters, and negative values suggest misclassification.

Davies-Bouldin Index (DBI)

The DBI evaluates clustering quality by looking at the ratio between within-cluster scatter and between-cluster separation. Lower values indicate better clustering. A DBI close to 0 implies compact, well-separated clusters. High values suggest poor clustering structure.
